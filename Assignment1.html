Cox's Bazar


History


Ancient Name was "Palonkee".

The present name has derived from Captain Hiram Cox, who was an officer of British East India company.

It covers an area of 27km.sq (27 mahallas, 9 words).

It is connected by air and bus as a communication medium.

Cox's Bazar is mainly popular for tourism, fish, sandy sea beach.

Imagine yourself, you are driving in a place entangled with hills that touches the sea water. The waves that rush into the big pebbles. It has the scenic Inani beach that stretches to Teknaf zero point.In the last point you can relish the sight seen between Bangladesh and Myanmar. Naaf River divides Bangladesh and Myanmar. In between you can see the beautiful 5 star hotel Royal Tulip. Also, the restaurant called, "Coral Station" is beautiful. The hills looks like as you have painted the realm. In between you can find "Shampan" boat.

Hotel

Sayeman Beach Resort

After fifty years of glorious past, Sayeman Beach Resort revives its famed legacy of comfort, elegance and impeccable service. An eminent landmark constructed in 1964, this legendary first private hotel of Cox’s Bazar is reborn, infusing modern sophistication into this vintage-chic, iconic hotel at a new beachfront location of Marine Drive, Kolatoli, Cox’s Bazar.

Royal Tulip

Royal Tulip Sea Pearl Beach Resort & Spa is located on Inani beach, Cox's Bazar with lush green hills rise from the east and endless sea stretching on the west, the resort offers panoramic visuals of Bay of Bengal.

Jaltaranga

Only 10 minutes’ drive from the Cox’s Bazar airport, located in the heart of Laboni Beach, Jol Tarongo welcomes you to an unforgettable experience and world class comfort for you and your family.
